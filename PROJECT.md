Catalyst Project (Background)
Open Knowledge is launching a new initiative to develop workshops and learning
resources collaboratively with four social sector organisations in four countries.
These will be organisations who are asking crucial questions about the risks and
opportunities of data and AI—both for their own work and for their constituents.
Their concerns span from technical to the social, and Open Knowledge will help
catalyse learning and discussion towards answers, supported by thematic
specialists around the world.

AI Learning Labs: “How do we build a Climate Academy
Chatbot?”

Open Knowledge, Climate Academy, and Semantic Climate are collaborating on
an open learning initiative to answer one specific, challenging question about AI:
How do we train a responsible AI chatbot to help counter misinformation about
climate change?

The organization: Climate Academy is a small but mighty organization that
educates young people and media on how to understand climate justice and
planetary boundaries.

The problem: Distortion and misrepresentation about climate change is prolific
in the media and in mainstream AI chatbots. People use AI all the time, but don’t
necessarily know how to question what they are told. How can climate educators
leverage AI?

The opportunity: Climate Academy wishes to disseminate knowledge with a
responsible and climate-friendly AI chatbot that can answer questions about
climate change using pre-approved literature, datasets and conversational
guardrails.
